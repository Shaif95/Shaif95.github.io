Course Description & Prerequisites

Fundamental concepts of intelligent computer systems, knowledge representation, logical reasoning, search strategies, game theory, Bayesian networks, neural networks, reinforcement learning, natural language processing, and current research in AI area.

Prerequisite: Graduate standing in computer science

Textbooks

Required

Stuart Russell and Peter Norvig, Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach, Prentice-Hall, 3rd Edition. ISBN-13: 978-0134610993, ISBN-10: 0134610997

Recommended

Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville, Deep Learning, MIT Press, 2016 — deeplearningbook.org

This class will also draw material from lecture slides of Magdon-Ismail, resources and slides available at deeplearningbook.org, the book Dive into Deep Learning by Aston Zhang, Zachary C. Lipton, Mu Li, and Alexander J. Smola, as well as resources from Wikimedia.

Course & University Policies

Instructor’s Policies

Attendance

Attend regularly and participate in discussions and exercises. Notify the instructor in advance of planned absences for valid reasons.

Late/Missing Work/Extra Credit

Late assignments accepted up to one week after the posted due date, submitted in the specified manner.

Use of AI in the Classroom

AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT) are not permitted for quizzes or in-class exams. Their use in projects and homework is allowed; students are responsible for verifying AI-assisted work and using it responsibly.
